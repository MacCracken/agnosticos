## What It Does

- **Psychometrics**: Big Five measurement, Cronbach's alpha, item response theory, test reliability
- **Cognition**: Working memory (Baddeley), dual-process theory (System 1/2), cognitive load, attention models
- **Psychophysics**: Weber-Fechner Law, Stevens' Power Law, Fitts' Law, Hick's Law
- **Learning**: Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, spaced repetition, operant/classical conditioning schedules
- **Decision-making**: Prospect theory (Kahneman/Tversky), expected utility, bounded rationality, anchoring bias
- **Perception**: Signal detection theory (d-prime), Gestalt grouping principles
- **Development**: Piaget stages, Erikson psychosocial stages

## Relationship to Bhava

Bhava simulates how an individual *feels* (emotion, personality, mood). Bodh models the measurable *laws* of cognition, perception, and behavior. Bhava is affective computing; bodh is computational psychology.

## Consumers

- **kiran/joshua** — NPC cognition, player modeling, learning AI
- **agnosai** — decision-making models for AI agents
