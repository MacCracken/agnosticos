## What It Does

- **Era**: Historical periods with date ranges and civilizational phases (Bronze Age through Information Age). Year-based lookup.
- **Civilization**: Major civilizations with geographic extent, peak periods, key traits, associated scripts and languages. Region and year-based lookup.
- **Event**: Structured historical events with categories (war, treaty, discovery, invention, revolution). Causality chains planned.
- **Calendar**: Calendar system metadata — Gregorian, Julian, Islamic, Hebrew, Chinese, Hindu, Maya, Egyptian. Type classification (solar, lunar, lunisolar). Computation lives in sankhya; metadata lives here.
- **Figure**: Historical figures with era/civilization context and domain classification (ruler, philosopher, scientist, artist, etc.).

## Relationship to Sankhya

Sankhya owns the *mathematics* of ancient systems (Mayan Long Count arithmetic, Chinese sexagenary cycle computation, Vedic Katapayadi encoding). Itihas owns the *historical context* — when and where these systems were used, which civilizations developed them, what era they belong to.

## Relationship to Avatara

Avatara maps mythological archetypes to personality. Itihas provides the civilizational context — which pantheons belong to which civilizations and eras, grounding divine archetypes in historical reality.

## Consumers

- **sankhya** — historical context for ancient mathematical systems
- **avatara** — civilizational context for mythological archetypes
- **kiran/joshua** — era-appropriate settings, historical NPC generation
- **jnana** — knowledge corpus (historical facts as structured data)
- **lipi** — historical attestation dates for scripts and languages
- **vidya** — history of science and technology
